## Inspiration

We watched a special-ed teacher's reality up close: one room, kids reading anywhere from a 3rd- to a 6th-grade level, every one of them owed the same standard taught in a way they can actually reach. She was doing it — but it cost her every night and weekend. We wanted to give that time back.

## What it does

IEPrep turns one standard into a full week of lessons, differentiated for every learner in the room. Build your class once, pick your standards, and Claude writes day-by-day plans tailored to each student's needs. Then it turns any day into ready-to-teach Smart Board slides — and tracks your IEP goals along the way.

## How we built it

Next.js and TypeScript on the front, Claude Opus 4.8 doing the thinking. We gave Claude everything a teacher juggles — standards, disabilities, accommodations, IEP data — and let it plan, build slides, and recommend next steps. Wrapped in a warm little "garden" design, because hard work deserves a gentle place to happen. Deployed on Railway.

## Challenges we ran into

Plenty broke. The first version crammed a whole month into one slideshow. "Load class" did nothing for a frustrating hour. Tiny rounding bugs, a menu that wouldn't click, a deploy that kept failing. Each one was a small wall — and getting past them is what made it real.

## Accomplishments that we're proud of

It actually works, end to end. Not a mockup — a real teacher could open it tomorrow, load their class, and walk into the room with slides ready. We're proudest that it doesn't just spit out a plan; it goes all the way to the moment that matters: standing in front of kids.

## What we learned

The plan was never the hard part — it's everything *after* the plan. We learned to trust Claude with the full picture instead of clever prompts, and we learned that for someone this tired and this stretched, a calm, kind interface isn't a nice-to-have. It's the whole point.

## What's next for IEPrep

Saved accounts, gentle reminders before IEP deadlines, more states' standards, one-click progress reports for parent meetings, and richer slides. Mostly: more time handed back to the people who give all of theirs.
